About

I am a graduate student in the Department of Earth Sciences at the University of California, Santa Barbara and am currently advised by Dr. Robin Matoza. I am interested in the different ways we can learn about the Earth through sound. As an undergraduate student at the University of Maryland, College Park, I studied Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences and minored in Surficial Geology.

Before becoming a graduate student, I worked at NOAA’s National Environmental Satellite, Data, and Information Service – Center for Satellite Applications and Research (NESDIS/STAR) as an atmospheric scientist. Here, I learned how raw microwave and infrared satellite data would be assessed and processed for operational weather forecasting.
